Historic Milestones

75 Years of Democratic History

1947

Independence & Partition

On 15 August, India gained independence from British rule. The simultaneous partition into India and Pakistan displaced over 14 million people in one of history's largest mass migrations. Jawaharlal Nehru delivered his iconic "Tryst with Destiny" speech at midnight in Parliament.

Independence
1950

The Constitution Comes Into Force

On 26 January, the Constitution of India replaced the Government of India Act. Drafted by a constituent assembly led by Dr B.R. Ambedkar, it established India as a sovereign democratic republic with universal adult franchise — at the time one of the most ambitious democratic experiments ever attempted.

Constitution
1952

The First General Election

With 173 million eligible voters — most illiterate — India conducted its first Lok Sabha elections over four months. The Indian National Congress, led by Nehru, won a decisive majority. The world watched in disbelief: universal suffrage in a newly independent, largely rural nation.

Elections
1966

Indira Gandhi Becomes Prime Minister

Indira Gandhi was sworn in as India's first and only female Prime Minister, a position she would hold for three separate terms. Her tenure reshaped Indian politics — marked by the nationalisation of banks, the 1971 Bangladesh Liberation War, and the controversial declaration of Emergency in 1975.

Leadership
1975

The Emergency (1975–1977)

Citing internal disturbance, Prime Minister Indira Gandhi declared a State of Emergency, suspending civil liberties and press freedom for 21 months. Thousands of opposition leaders were imprisoned. The Emergency remains one of the most controversial chapters in Indian democratic history.

Civil Liberties
1977

India's First Transfer of Power

The post-Emergency election saw the Indian National Congress defeated for the first time. The Janata Party coalition, uniting disparate opposition forces, came to power under Morarji Desai — proving India's democratic institutions could survive authoritarian stress.

Democracy
1991

Economic Liberalisation

Facing a balance-of-payments crisis, Finance Minister Manmohan Singh under PM Narasimha Rao implemented sweeping economic reforms — dismantling the Licence Raj, opening India to foreign investment, and setting the stage for decades of rapid growth. A turning point in India's modern political economy.

Economy
2014

The BJP's Historic Majority

Narendra Modi led the Bharatiya Janata Party to a landmark majority — the first single-party majority in 30 years. The election saw the highest voter turnout (66.4%) in India's history to that point, and marked a shift in Indian political dynamics toward Hindu nationalist politics and development-focused campaigning.

Elections
2024

The World's Largest Election

With 968 million registered voters, the 2024 Lok Sabha election was the largest democratic exercise in human history. Conducted across 7 phases over 44 days, the BJP-led NDA secured a third consecutive term, though with a reduced majority — requiring coalition partners for government formation.

2024
People's Movements

Defining Political Movements

✊

Non-Cooperation Movement

Gandhi's call to Indians to withdraw from British institutions through peaceful non-compliance — boycotting courts, schools, and colonial goods — mobilised millions and fundamentally altered the terms of colonial rule.

1920 – 1922
🧂

Salt March (Dandi March)

Gandhi's 386-km march to the sea to make salt in defiance of British tax law became a global symbol of nonviolent resistance and galvanised the independence movement like no event before it.

1930
🚪

Quit India Movement

Gandhi's "Do or Die" call for immediate British withdrawal from India led to mass arrests, including of the entire Congress leadership, and demonstrated that colonial rule could no longer command consent.

1942
⚖️

Dalit Rights Movement

Dr B.R. Ambedkar's lifelong struggle against caste discrimination culminated in constitutionally enshrined protections for Scheduled Castes and Tribes, reservations in public institutions, and the abolition of untouchability.

1920s – 1956
🌱

Chipko Movement

Women in the Himalayan foothills literally embraced trees to prevent logging, spawning one of India's earliest and most influential environmental movements and inspiring ecological activism globally.

1973
📜

RTI Movement

The grassroots campaign for the Right to Information, led by activists including Aruna Roy and the MKSS in Rajasthan, culminated in the landmark RTI Act 2005 — transforming government accountability in India.

1990s – 2005
